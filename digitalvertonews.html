The news article "Cyber Monday tips from a cyber security guru" was published in the Standard Examiner website, and it's reported that the period between Thanksgiving and Christmas is when consumers use their credit cards or debit cards at online stores or physical stores the most throughout the year. That's when stores make at least 20% of their money, and it's also a time when a lot of credit card and debit card information is breached and stolen. The author suggests to only enter your real information in online stores that are well known, and if you want to support a small business, then make sure you use a credit card instead of a debit card. Also make sure that you pay with a card that has a chip, and don't swipe your card at small business stores.
