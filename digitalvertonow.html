I do this kind of work for free from home, and I also work for East Salinas Building Healthy Communities in Monterey County as a CHW. I aspire to make a website for everyone on this planet, so they can represent themselves in the digital world. At Building Healthy Communities, my current function is to educate Monterey county individuals on the COVID-19 resources that they have. At this time, there's 3 active programs in the county for the homeless, agriculture workers, and healthcare workers to isolate and quarantine in a hotel room. The first program's called Project Roomkey, the second program's called Housing for the Harvest, and the third program's called Non-Congregate Sheltering for California Healthcare Workers. Individuals isolating or quarantining need food to be delivered to their residencies, and we provide that service for them as well. Every city has a food distribution day, and you should call your local food bank to find out which day and what time the food distribution is. The United Farm Workers Foundation has a Salinas office that distributes food every Wednesday from 3pm-5:30pm. I've volunteered there before, and volunteers with larger families are allowed a greater quantity of food boxes to be taken home. East Salinas Building Healthy Communities also provides relief funds to Monterey county residents, so they can pay off their utility bills, buy food, or rent bills. My days at work mostly consist of chatting with immagrants at La Princesa Merkets in East Salinas and at La Plaza Bakery in East Salinas as well. I've expressed that we need to move on to other outreach locations like the Northridge Mall and movie theatres. The immigrants have migrated to Salinas to work in the agriculture and food industries, and a lot of them are illiterate to the point where they can't read or write their own language, Spanish. Also, some residents display terrible interest in these resources, and it could be because they don't want to give out their name, contact information, and living situation to strangers. The immigrant workers may also think that these resources cost money and don't want to spend the small quantity of money they earn. It takes me about 5 seconds to ask these indivudals if they want to hear about our COVID 19 resources, so it's critical I say key words clearly and quickly to clinch their attention. Twenty-five percent of the individuals I've approached declined to hear about the resources, 25% listen to me, and the other 50% say that they're already vaccinated and aware of the resources. The 3 locations where I've done outreach at have generally increased their positive response in which it's a great pleasure to converse about the individual's reactions to the COVID-19 vaccines and their COVID-19 Relief Funds. A strawberry worker promised to bring me some strawberries, but he's yet to do so. Our efforts have surely been translating into a positive impact, and we've vaccinated residents at over twice the rate of the entire nation.
